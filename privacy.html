Privacy Policy for Mono

Last Updated: April 22, 2026

I built Mono because I wanted a better way to organise my own video collection without compromising my privacy. This is a solo and very personal venture. Because of that, I’ve designed it with the same unreasonably high standards I hold every other app to: "Leave my data alone."

1. No Data Leaves Your Machine

Mono is a local-only application. No cloud, no backend server, and no "phone home" feature. Your sources, library, your watch history, and your tags live exclusively on your computer in a private SQLite database.

2. Access to Your Files

For you to organise and watch your videos, Mono needs permission to read the folders you explicitly import.

  • What it can access: The specific folders and video files you choose to add to the library.
  • What it can't access: The rest of your hard drive.

3. Metadata and Storage

Any information you add to the app (e.g. video descriptions, custom tags, or playback history) is stored in a private SQLite database file located on your computer. You have full control over this file; if you delete the app or the database file, all that metadata is permanently erased from your machine.

4. Third-Party Services

I do not use tracking pixels, analytics software, or third-party advertising services (not even a cookie).

5. Updates

Because I don't collect your email address or personal contact information, I can't contact you directly regarding updates. Please check monoapp.uk periodically for the latest version of the application.
